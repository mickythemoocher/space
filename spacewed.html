THE DESERT SLAND ADVENTURE

Characters

Peter

Betty

Charlie

Mickey

Steph

Mummy

Peterr: Look! Look! The ship. It is almost all under water now.

Charlie: Oh my gosh, we were so lucky to swim to this island.

Steph: It was such a long swim.

Mickey: Yes it was but we all made it.

Betty: I am exhausted after swimming so far.

Charlie: Me too but now we can relax on this beautiful beach for a while.

Peter: Aren't we lucky to have been so close to this beautiful island.

Steph: It is but do you think it will take long till we are rescued

Peter: No way of knowing that Steph.

Charlie: People will know that the ship sank so hopefully they will soon start looking for us.

Betty: So we could be stuck here all night?

Peter: Actually Betty it could b much longer than that.

Betty: How long Peter?

Peter: It really is too difficult to say. Charlie: Peter is right. We could actually be here for quite a while.

Micky: well we won't freeze that's for sure. It is very warm.

Steph: Yes the weather is lovely.

Micky: Peter, if you are right and we could be here for a while, we should try to get organised.

Betty: What do you mean? Organised? What is there to organise?

Peter: Micky is right. If we are here for a while we need to fend for ourselves. There are no adults to look after us.

Betty: I see what you mean.

Charlie: Yes, this is true. We need food, water and shelter at least.

Steph: Of course! We are so used to our mums and dads doing things like that but now we have to do it for ourselves.

Micky: So we can't just lie here on this beach all day.

Peter: That's true, by nightfall we will definitely need food water and shelter.

Betty: so we need to explore the island then

Steph: Yes I suppose that would be the first thing to do.

Peter: I think we ought to organise other things first.

Micky: What do you mean Peter, surely they are the most important things.

Peter: Yes, they are important but unless we are careful we will be approaching this in the wrong way.

Betty: What do you mean by that?

Peter: What I mean is we don't want to all go off willy nilly looking for things without organising ourselves properly first. Otherwise I think we will just be falling over each other and nobody knowing what they should be doing

Micky: Ok, that is quite a good point.

Steph: Yes it is. So what do you suggest Peter?

Peter: This may sound a little crazy but as you can see we appear to have this island all to ourselves.

Charlie: Yes, it does seem that way.

Betty: So what are you suggesting Peter?

Peter: We arrived here by accident but really, now, It is like our own country?

Mickey: Oh yes! It is

Steph: Yes, how exciting.

Betty: It is exciting but what does it mean?

Peter: Well all countries have governments don't they?

Charlie: Yes, that is true.

Steph: So are you saying we should have a government Peter: Exactly. That is what I meant by organising ourselves.

Micky: Ok, I get it but a government is elected by the people of the country, and we are the only people.

Peter: That is true but I still think we can be responsible for different things.

Betty: That sounds like a good idea

Steph: Maybe difficult to do though.

Peter: I don't think so. You see, all governments have ministers. They are people that take charge of different departments to help the country run smoothly.

Micky: Yes, I have heard that.

Betty: So what ministers would we need in our new country?

Peter: I have already thought about this. Firstly there would be the Prime Minister. He is in charge of all the others.

Steph: The top job?

Peter: Yes.

Micky: What others would there be in our country?

Peter: I was thinking a minister for food, one for shelter, one for water and one to concentrate on rescue.

Betty: And of course the Prime Minister

Peter: Yes.

Steph: So a role for all five of us?

Peter: Exactly.

Micky: So how would we decide who has what role?

Peter: That would be the difficult part.

Charlie: I guess everyone would want the top job?

Steph: Of course!

Peter: I though the easiest way was to elect a Prime Minister first and he would decide who the others would be.

Charlie: That seems like a good idea.

Betty: So how do we elect the Prime Minister? Anyone have any ideas?

Charlie: I think the eldest amongst us should have that role.

Steph: I would agree with that?

Betty: Me too.

Micky: Peter, you are the eldest, will you be prime minister?

Peter: Of course, I will.

Betty: So who will be your new ministers Peter?

Peter: Well let me see now, this may not be an easy task.

Charlie: Maybe we could choose which one we want to be?

Steph: That's a good idea

Peter: It would be as long as everyone doesn't want the same post

Micky: That's true.

Peter: I have an idea though, I will ask for volunteers for each role.

Charlie: Yes! That could work.

Peter: So let's see if we can do it this way?

Betty: This is exciting!

Peter: So let's start with the minister of food. Would anyone like that post?

Steph: I think I would be good at that. My mum likes to pick things from the woods near our house, like mushrooms and berries and I have helped her do that.

Peter: Wicked! Just what we want. So Steph you are the new minister for food?

Charlie: We can't survive on just mushrooms and berries though.

Steph: Of course not but last year my dad taught me to fish so I will do that too.

Betty: Oh good! I love fish

Peter: Me too so I think you will be a great minister of food Steph.

Steph: Thank you.

Peter: Next is the minister of water. There is plenty of sea water but of course we can't drink that.

Betty: Yuck!

Charlie: What exactly would the minister for water do Peter?

Peter: Mostly it will be exploring. Trying to find a river or lake on the island for fresh water.

Betty: That sounds a bit dangerous, who knows what is on this island.

Charlie: I'd like to be minister of water Peter: I am nmot afraid of exploring and I will be very careful to keep safe.

Peter. Very well then Charlie, you are the minister for water. Now even though we will all have our separate jobs, that doesn't mean we can't help each other so it maybe a good idea to take one ot he other with you.

Charlie: Ok I'll do that. See that big hill behind us? I think there could be water over there. Maybe a river flowing down the hill.

Steph: Good plan Charlie.

Peter: Next we need a minister for shelter. Something to protect us a bit from the cold and night and shelter us if it rains.

Mickey: My dad says I am quite good at building things, and I did once make a camp in our garden.

Peter: Ok Micky the job is yours. I think it will be a very tricky job, so as prime minister, I will help you with that.

Mickey: Ok peter that sounds good.

Betty: As all the other posts have been taken I will be minister of rescue then?

Peter: Are you happy with that Betty?

Betty: Yes, I have ideas already.

Steph: Wow! That was quick Betty. What are your ideas?

Charlie: Yes tell us how will we be rescued?

Betty: The first thing I will do is collect lots of pebbles from the beach, as large as possible, and lay them out to spell 'HELP'. If a plane fries over hopefully they will see it and send someone to rescue us.

Peter: Great idea Betty

Charlie: Yes! That sounds like just what we need.

Betty: Also I need to gather some wood and dry bushes to build a big fire. Someone may see either the fames or even the smoke.

Mickey: Another good idea Betty.

Betty: Trouble is we probably don't have anything to light the fire with.

Charlie: I can help with this, look, I have a magnifying glass in my pocket

Mickey: Yes, that would do it.

Charlie: We can direct some sunlight onto some dry twigs and it should get hot enough to start fire

Betty: Oh great! Will you help me with that then Charlie?

Charlie: Yes, of course.

Peter: Well that was so much easier than I expected. There is one more thing that we should do though.

Steph: What's that Peter?

Peter: We have a brand new government in a brand new country. But our country hasn't got a name. What shall we call it.

Betty: Oh gosh! Yes, we have to give our new country a name.

Charlie: Something grand and important.

Steph: Yes, but not too long though, something we can all remember.

Mickey:A name we can be proud of.

Peter: As Prime Minister may I suggested – Makanrushi?

Mickey: I like that, it has a good ring to it.

Betty: It sounds very grand.

Charlie:I think that is a winner Peter.

Steph: How did you think of that?

Peter: At school one day we had a geography lesson about uninhabited islands in the South Pacific. Places where nobody lives. Who knows? This could be that very island,

Steph: Great choice. Here's to our new country, Makanrushi.

Peter: So let's get to work – Oh oh – too late, look who I can see?

Mummy: Peter, Betty, come on in, it's time for tea.

Betty: Oh mum! We were having such a fun game can we stay a little longer?

Mummy. No dears, it will be dark soon, so say goodbye to your friends and come in for your tea

Peter: Ok mum, Goodbye everyone. (They all call out goodbyes)

THE END
